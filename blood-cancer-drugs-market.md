<p><ul><li>Blood Chemotherapy Drugs</li><li>Blood Targeted Therapy Drugs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The blood cancer drugs market is primarily classified into two types: blood chemotherapy drugs and blood targeted therapy drugs. Blood chemotherapy drugs work by indiscriminately targeting rapidly dividing cancer cells, disrupting their growth and proliferation. In contrast, blood targeted therapy drugs are designed to specifically target and inhibit the growth of cancer cells, often focusing on specific genetic mutations or molecular markers. Together, these treatments aim to improve patient outcomes by effectively managing various types of blood cancers, like leukemia and lymphoma.</p></p>
