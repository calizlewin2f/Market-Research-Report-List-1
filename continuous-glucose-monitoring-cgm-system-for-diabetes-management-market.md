<p><p>Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) systems for diabetes management are categorized into two main types: wearable and implantable devices. Wearable CGMs are external devices, typically attached to the skin, that provide real-time glucose readings through sensors. They are user-friendly and easily replaceable. In contrast, implantable CGMs are inserted under the skin and offer longer-term glucose monitoring, requiring less frequent maintenance. Both types aim to improve glucose control and enhance patient quality of life by providing continuous data and trends.</p></p>
